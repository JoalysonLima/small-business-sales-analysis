# Business Insights and Recommendations

This document summarizes the main business insights generated from the cleaned sales dataset.

## Overall Performance

**Insight:** The business generated a solid level of net revenue across the analysed period.

**Evidence:** Total net revenue was £450,105, from 4,994 orders, with an average order value of £90.

**Business Interpretation:** The business can monitor performance using simple KPIs such as revenue, orders, average order value, units sold, and discounts.

**Recommendation:** Create a simple monthly KPI dashboard to track net revenue, orders, average order value, discounts, and units sold.

**Priority:** High

---

## Monthly Performance

**Insight:** The strongest month was 2025-04, while the weakest month was 2025-06.

**Evidence:** 2025-04 generated £41,686; 2025-06 generated £33,635.

**Business Interpretation:** Monthly variation suggests that sales performance should be monitored regularly. The business should investigate what happened during stronger and weaker months.

**Recommendation:** Review sales activity in 2025-04 and 2025-06 to identify possible drivers of stronger and weaker performance.

**Priority:** Medium

---

## Category Performance

**Insight:** Outerwear was the leading category by net revenue.

**Evidence:** Outerwear represented 39.94% of total net revenue.

**Business Interpretation:** The leading category is a key revenue driver. In this dataset, category performance is influenced not only by sales volume, but also by product price level.

**Recommendation:** Prioritize Outerwear in stock planning and product visibility, while monitoring dependency on this category.

**Priority:** High

---

## Product Performance

**Insight:** Winter Jacket was the strongest product by net revenue.

**Evidence:** Winter Jacket represented 21.54% of total net revenue.

**Business Interpretation:** The top product is commercially important and should receive attention in inventory planning, pricing, and product visibility.

**Recommendation:** Ensure Winter Jacket remains consistently available and visible across the strongest channels.

**Priority:** High

---

## Product Concentration

**Insight:** The top 5 products represented 78.58% of total net revenue.

**Evidence:** The top 3 products represented 56.06% and the top 5 products represented 78.58% of total net revenue.

**Business Interpretation:** Revenue is highly concentrated in a small number of products. This is positive because these products are strong performers, but it also creates dependency risk.

**Recommendation:** Protect the top-selling products, but test ways to grow secondary products to reduce revenue concentration risk.

**Priority:** High

---

## Sales Channel

**Insight:** Physical Store was the strongest sales channel.

**Evidence:** Physical Store represented 44.43% of total net revenue.

**Business Interpretation:** The strongest channel should be prioritized in sales planning, but the business should also compare operational costs before shifting investment.

**Recommendation:** Review why Physical Store performs well and consider prioritizing it in future campaigns.

**Priority:** High

---

## Location

**Insight:** Glasgow generated the highest revenue among all cities.

**Evidence:** Glasgow represented 12.63% of total net revenue.

**Business Interpretation:** The top city may indicate stronger local demand, better customer fit, or stronger order volume in that location.

**Recommendation:** Consider targeted campaigns or local offers for customers in Glasgow.

**Priority:** Medium

---

## Discounts

**Insight:** Discounted orders represented 54.92% of total net revenue.

**Evidence:** Total discounts reached £35,065, with an overall discount rate of 7.23%. Discounted orders had an AOV of £86, while non-discounted orders had an AOV of £96.

**Business Interpretation:** Discounted orders contribute meaningfully to revenue, but their average order value is lower than non-discounted orders. Discounts should therefore be reviewed carefully.

**Recommendation:** Do not increase discounts automatically. First compare margin, order volume, and average order value between discounted and non-discounted orders.

**Priority:** High

---

## Payment Methods

**Insight:** Cash was the most used payment method by number of orders.

**Evidence:** Cash represented 21.15% of all orders.

**Business Interpretation:** Payment preferences are relatively balanced, so the business should maintain multiple payment options instead of over-optimizing for only one method.

**Recommendation:** Keep all major payment methods available, since customer payment behavior is not concentrated in a single option.

**Priority:** Low
